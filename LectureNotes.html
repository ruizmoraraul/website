Set Theory and Forcing

Introduction to Modal Logic

Definition of basic notions of modal propositional logic as syntax, relational semantics, models and frames, bisimulations, model theoretic and frame theoretic constructions, completeness. More advanced topics including expressive power and neighbourhood frames.

Mathematical Structures in Logic

Study of duality theories for Boolean algebras, Heyting algebras, distributive lattices, modal algebras and other algebraic structures appearing in logic. Consequences of these results for corresponding logics in terms of completeness, the finite model property, axiomatizations, decidability, interpolation etc. The classes of intermediate, substructural, modal and other non-classical logics is also considered.
